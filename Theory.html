Theory

Metallography is the study of metals and alloys, Primariy their microstructures. The application of metallography mainly include observation, ofter via microscopy to determine the metal's chemical and microstructure and the distribution of the components or the phases.

In order to obscave or study the metallopraphic features of a given sample of metel or alloy, we need to prepare the specimen through some processes. The processes for ivolved are namely,

1) Sectioning /Cutting

2) Mounting, 

3) Grinding

4) Polishing

5) Etching

6) Metallographic Observation

  • Sectioning/Cutting
Metallographic cutting as sectioning actually marks the flast step of the metallographic sample /specimen preparation process. In most cases it is necessary because the part or solid body is too large for following metallegraphic processes carried out on a laboratory scale. The sectioning should be done, so that the specimen that we got after cutting should represent the whole part of substance that we have taken.

Tools requided for cutting a sectioning are:
* Hack Saw
* High Speed Saw
* Abrasive Wheel Cutters
* Low speed saw 
  • Mounting
The primary propose of mounting metallographic somples is that convenience in handling samples of difficult shapes or sizes duringg the subsequent steps for metallographic preparation and examination. A secondaay purpose is to protect and preserve extreme edges as surface defects during metallgraphic preparation.

  • Grinding
The purpose of grinding is to remove damaged layer, the uneven surface and the oxide layer that might have formed during the cutting operation. This should be done sequentially starting from the coarser one to the finer ene. Grinding should commence with the course size for making initial flat surface and remove  the effects of sectioning.

To ensure the complete elimination of the previous grinding scratches found by visual inspection, the direction of grinding must be changed by 90 from the one stage of grinding to the next stage.

And the grinding media that we have are,

SIC (silicon carbide)

AL2o3 (Aluminum oxide)

Al2O3-Fe3O4 

The tools the we use for grinding generally are
- Abrasive belt grinder
- Emery papers & fine soad paper.
  • Polishing
  • Rough Polishing
  • Final Polishing
Polishing is the process of creating a smooth and shiny surface by rubbing it or by applying a chemical teatment leaving a clean surface with a significant specular reflection. In other words, the process is to remove the damage caused by cutting and planar grinding
During this, the specimen is moved in a clockwise direction and the polishing wheel to ensure equal metal removal from the other surface, but not allowing prolonged polishing in any one direction. Diamond compound abrasives or suspension are used for this kind of process.
This process is used to remove the surface damage and not the damage caused by cutting and planar grinding. If the damage from the steps is not removed completely, the rough polishing should be repeated before moving on to the final layer of surface for analysis. Alumina suspension, colloidal silica Suspensions are use for this final polishing process.
  • Etching
Etching is a process that enhances the contrast on surfaces in order to visualize the microstructures. In other words, it is done to optically enhance microstructural features of the material. A suitabe chemical mixture is applied on the flat surface to reveal the micostucture. Etchants reveal the structure by either preferentially attacking grain boundries or preferentially staining grains of different orientations. Etching is done in petri dishes or in other suitable containers with loose covers to prevent excessive evaporation of the solvent, particularly alcohol solutions.
We have different types of etching techniques.
They are:-

Emersion
Electrolyte
