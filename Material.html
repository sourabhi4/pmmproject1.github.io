Chemicals For Etching

1. First and foremost, take the given metal or alloy and obtain the sample of it by operation of            cutting/sectioning using appropriate cutting tools like hack saw, abrasive cutter etc.
2. Now based on the properties of given sample, either go with cold or hot mounting.
3. After the mounting, grinde the given sample using belt grinder, emery paper etc. This should be        done sequentially starting from coarser one to finer one. 
4. To obtain the smooth & mirror like finish, polish the specimen by moving it in a clockwise                  direction around the polishing wheel.
5. Then etch the specimen using the appropriate etchants (Chemicals) to enhance the contrast on        surfaces.
6. Finally, the specimen is prepared and ready for the metallographic observation.

Precautions

  • Care should be taken, while performing cutting, grinding operations.
  • Make sure that gloves should be used as chemicals are involved in the processes.
  • Ensure that the sample should be washed with water & dried everytime after the completion of grinding, polishing operation. 

Conclusion

  • Thus, we are able to prepare the specimen by following the given precautions & procedure.
  • The etched specimen can be magnified under a microscope  & the microstructure (without any distortions) of specimen can be seen.
